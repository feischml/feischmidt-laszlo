Read

Learning equals moving forward. Reading is the most important part of the process and when it becomes a daily routine, it will be the most powerfull. Here are some of the books that impacted my perception about life, relationships, work:

John C. Maxwell

The 15 Invaluable Laws of Growth

Development does not appear "just like that". As much as you delay doing something, the greater are the chances that you will never do it. Take action. Today.

Watch how John Maxwell is explaining the law of reflection here.

Ray Dalio

Principles

A lot of invaluable information and guidence. Life and work principles in one book for achieving one's goals and making effective choices - a mix of psychology and neuroscience applied and greatly exemplified.

Ray Dalio on radical truth and transparency: here.

Angela Duckworth

Grit

What is more important? Hard work, effort or talent? Angela describes why effort counts twice and how small, achievable, daily goals can keep the motivation level up and help in making progress.

Have a look here at the Ted talk about Grit.

Simon Sinek

Start with WHY

We have to understand our WHY. Anyone can explain what he/she is doing, organisations can explain what they are doing, but only a few can answer to the question WHY? WHY is the thing that inspires us and the people around us. 

Great interview with Simon Sinek talking about leadership and millenials  here.
